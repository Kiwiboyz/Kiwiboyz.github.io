Meals on wheels project

One of the hardest projects to work with so far

About

Whats the project?

We picked up this project about four weeks after everyone else had already started so we were already at a disadvantage. not to mention the project being drastically out of date and using old technologies it was like trying to sort out cables for a network without unplugging anything.

The project was supposed to be an easy system to allow admins to enter customer data in and show what that customer had ordered and when they wanted it delivered it even featured an integrated map that allowed the admin to see who the closest driver was to the drop off location.

Summary

In the end we had just fixed most of the previous errors and changed all the Agile code to Java this still wasn't enough for release we even had a whole new site in the making. with a fresh modern look that was user friendly and responsive

unfortunately we ran out of time and did not get to integrate the new site which is what I primarily worked on the site was added to the git repository so that hopefully a future team can take over and finish what was left of our work.

All in all, I feel we did a great job considering out circumstances and issues we had along the way I really enjoyed working with the team I had and hope to work with them again in the future the next project will be completed.

Reflection

Even though this is reflected in my essay I thought I’d add a summarised reflection here. As the semester comes to a close and the project comes to an end I feel somewhat sad that it wasn’t completely finished we faced a lot of issues over this semester from having no client and being given a project that not even the original team knew what was truly going on.
We faced a lot of issues and fought each one as a team. We may not have finished the project and may not have had any real lessons in this class but I find it was also one of the most valuable class's I have ever been in. The environment was very much like the real world we had no specific rules and we had to rely on others to get passed certain tasks.
Looking back on this semester I definitely have learnt a fair amount about how to work in a team and how essential team communication is. There were several things that my gut instinct told me to do but being put in the situation of having no idea of where to start or what was what was truly the best way to experiences what a bad project is like. I feel that this would be a challenge for even people already in the workforce with learning new technologies accompanied by the strict timeline broken system and little information to work with.
Overall I’m happy with what we achieved as a team even if the project wasn't fully completed I fell that we achieved more overall and the skills that we picked up along the way are invaluable.
